Immortal Jellyfish

These small animals do not die after maturity. Instead, they transform to an early stage of growth and start their life cycle from that stage again

  • Scientific Name: Turritopsis Dohrnii
  • Average Length: 4.5 mm
  • Average Lifespan: ∞
  • Habitat: Mediterranean Sea and waters of Japan

They are bell-shaped with a length equal to their width. While an adult Dohrnii has a maximum diameter of about 4.5 mm, a young one is as small as 1 mm.

Stages and the life cycle of a Dohrnii can be summarized as follows: Fertilized egg, planula larvae (settle onto the sea-floor and grow there), polyp (or hydroids - still on the sea-floor), young jellyfish (now they in the plankton where they grow and feed), sexually mature jellyfish, polyp, young jellyfish, mature jellyfish, polyp, and goes on...
